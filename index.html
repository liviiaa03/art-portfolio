Artist Statement

These paintings harbor a disquieting vulnerability—one that unsettles yet demands empathy. In my paintings, wounds do not sit on the skin, nor do they scream for immediate attention. Instead, they seep from within, bleeding through the eyes, ears, and organs —slow, persistent, and inescapable. The muted palette and soft, hazy forms mirror the suppression of pain, yet the stark presence of raw, red forms and drips punctuate the composition and refuse to be ignored. By resisting quiet conformity, they embody a paradox of fragility and defiance.

This series is a meditation on vulnerability—on the ways we internalize pain, the ways it manifests when it can no longer be contained. It is about the quiet violence of suffering that is not seen, the weight of wounds that have no visible scars. The flock does no t blend into silence—it bleeds, it weeps, and in doing so, it asserts its presence.
